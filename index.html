"--that said person returned with the most fiery of passions--" he sounds amused now, tickled by the ever-changing emotion that paints her visage -- conscpicuous interest, skepticism, and a return to disgust which was an expected eventuality.

"--but all she will ever remember is their mutual, unending hatred for each other."

He would act similarly if he was in her shoes. Probably call her names -- a lunatic, a charlatan, or an occultist.

"With good reason, might I add. Although obviously, I don't loathe you as much."

"I killed your friends. I corrupted the world as you know it. I reveled in it all."

James delivers the line with impeccable matter-of-factliness. Free of stutter, cadence steady as seconds ticking by. He regrets nothing, and it could not be more apparent to Emma.
"I lost--" he halts his train of thought and attempts to start anew.

The pause gives her a window of opportunity, and out of nowhere Emma punches him square in the face, a thwack that sends him a few steps backwards. The jab, he realises has hurt her knuckles as her sudden grimace betrays her strength. James backs up some more, instinctively away from her, clutching his bleeding and very broken nose. She wastes no time as he fixes its alignment and scrambles for the gun he flung earlier towards the nursery's corner. She succeeds in retrieving it, and they return to square one, the Anderson Wheeler Mark VII pointing at ignorant infant-James, and then back towards his skull.

"Good choice, my sweet," he goads.

He hears it click.

Too many times in succession.

It brings him no pain. He is unharmed. Athough he feels... he feels...

Time to stop playing games, and put his foot down.

"You died. You don't exist in my world anymore."

Emma is unfazed by the news. He expected no less of a reaction. He wishes he was this brave, oh, only if he were a lesser man, but on second thought, immortality is the bane of his existence.

James sits on a stool with uneven legs by the door, and motions for her to do the same with the gaudy armchair decked in geometric print across the floor. He fishes out a white, pristine handkerchief from his pant pocket, its three initials visible on its lower right corner, and wipes the red dripping down his philtrum. Her eyes spot the insignia, and they lose some of their unyielding hardness but she remains rooted on the spot, and pulls the trigger again. He hears the firearm crackle once more, this time a little louder. Still, no bullet exits the revolver. That's the seventh chamber, he counts. The cylinder is, was not loaded, not even blanks. She keeps her aim steady, despite the gun's obvious inability to pose a real threat to any soul in the room. Infant-Lloyd is still whinging about, acting as background noise to offset the sounds of chrome-molybdenum steel cylinders revolving.

But he... he feels like he's been shot, and is dying a slow, aching death by the hands of his lover. His next words sound pained, his breathing uneven, laboured. He believes it to be the aftereffects of shattered nasal cavities.

"I'm just here to set myself up for failure... and witness the woman I long for and wish to possess,"

He waves his free hand and snaps his fingers twice, "disappear without a trace for the thousandth time."

Emma lowers her gun with reticence, and appears relenting to a cease-fire, but rapidly hurls it at him that its muzzle almost hits his nose -- again -- if he wasn't so agile to dodge the offending item. It leaves a small dent on the wall. Mrs. Tennyson will not like that, just as she despises everything that graces her presence. As he recovers from the surprise attack, James notices her hands resting on her hips, the right still holding on to the bloodied article. He interprets it as an act of surrender however hostile; he has her full attention and she is waiting for his next words to come.

"So as I was saying, I've tried to let you go, for more instances than I'd like to admit." James keeps going, and nothing can stop him. He's opened Pandora's box, and he has no energy left to resist unleashing his vulnerabilities.

"But I find, much to my satisfaction or dissatisfaction, I'm not certain which is stronger, that I simply... cannot. I keep returning here. You teach me something new about myself every day, dear Minister."

"Although as fate or luck would have it, I am the only one left with the burden of memory -- our memories. But let it be known that there is no... love lost between us. Just fondness. Familiarity." And with this James suspects, that it is not only the wretched woman's heart that he is trying to appeal to.

"What else can I exactly do with ten minutes anyway?"

He finishes his monologue, and laughs at the incredulousness of it all, and it echoes off the walls of the room, mocking him, as if the beginnings of a sneer on her wasn't enough.

Silence, now, saturates him with poison. He cannot sense the churning in his head anymore.

And their time, their time is drawing to a close.

"Say something," James begs commands her. He's doubtful if he can accept futility on his attempt number, well, any numerical figure including the number seven. But luck, he presumes, is firmly encamped on his side today.

Emma finally sits down, chest heaving, and opens her mouth wide enough to speak. She freezes for a beat, a period so interminable he swears it feels like a minute that extended to a hour in a hot second. The hesitation allows James to mentally conjure a garter snake, slithering down her insides and making its nest in her belly. Devouring her from the within. Or influencing her every action, mainly coercing her to bite the forbidden apple. He means no ill will, but gets a kick out of giving her imaginary torment in retaliation to her physical manhandling of him.

These thoughts are just the usual workings of his perverse imagination.

"Good for you," she scoffs, having finally found her voice.

How anticlimactic of a response, he muses.

She stands up and makes her way towards him, purposefully slowing down her steps, but stops just a half-foot before the edges of his scuffed brogues. She dips her head down and leans into his bloodied face, examining the stained piece of cloth with vested interest.

Emma snatches it away from his fingers and turns her back on him, all in lightning speed. Both her hands seem to be busy unfolding the pocket square, but his line of vision is obstructed by her slender back. What he is certain of, is that she will realise the fine embroidery spells out E. J. L., as she suspected earlier. He catches a glimpse of her knuckle joints, and they are rosy, and he swears by the moon, that the sides of her cheeks are of the same colouring.

"And to answer your question, what you could do..." She starts articulating clearly, as if making sure he hears every single syllable that rolls off her tongue,

"I suppose you can kill yourself -- and help us all."

She spins about face, beaming at him with her canines, and her eyes teeming with maniacal schaudenfreude.

There. The smile he has long waited for, not borne out of love or joy, but not displeasing. He cannot determine what he should feel on his own, but the expression warms his fingertips just enough to forgo leather gloves and still escape frostbites from the chilly December nighttime weather. It reaches his toes, and James reckons he can lose the socks too. She'd like that.

Par for the course, Emma, he humours himself.

And without another second to spare, she vanishes into thin air. The square settles to the parqueted floor, gathers what he assumes is dust and mold, and the beginnings of what he will learn to name as despair, tinged with sufferance later.

He picks it up and inhales its scent, amid the clotting blood blocking his nostrils.

Nothing. He smells nothing.

The longing does not go away.

He glances over his shoulder and finds newly-orphaned-James staring back at him with an eager curiosity.

Adult-James sighs in exasperation. "I suggest, that you shut your mouth when I speak. This... won't be my last trip."

He sets the desk lamp to dim, crosses the room and switches the overhead light off.

"I have half a mind to murder you just to make her happy."

Infant-James faux-giggles in delight, as if he understands his anguished mutterings.

The older Lloyd strides towards the nursery's entryway and shuts the door with a resounding clink, leaving his much younger self alone to rest in the darkness.

"You are as alone as I am, and ever will be," he whispers to no one.
